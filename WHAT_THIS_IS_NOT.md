# What this is not

Groundwork is a fast, transparent, evidence-linked **nowcast** of where one
kind of need appears to be moving. That is all it is. Specifically, it is:

- **Not an allocator.** No money flows through Groundwork. It publishes a map
  and an evidence chain; humans decide what to do with them.
- **Not impact-proven.** The fast clock (nowcast) is a *signal*, not proof.
  The slow clock (verified outcomes) is a separate layer with a separate
  confidence regime — and in v0 it is empty.
- **Not a ranking of which need "deserves" funding.** A higher nowcast_gap
  is not a moral ordering. It is a statement about evidence of movement.
- **Not a substitute for local knowledge.** Practitioners who work with 211
  and community data stress that people with local knowledge must participate
  in interpretation. Use the map *with* the people who know the place.
- **Not complete.** Under-resourced areas produce less data. Low coverage
  never means low need — check `coverage_score` and the bias register in
  `coverage/` before drawing conclusions about a quiet tract.
- **Not real-time truth.** Signals carry confidence, gameability discounts,
  and decay. Some sources can be gamed; those are down-weighted and labeled,
  not laundered.
